    To change the air filter in your car, locate the air filter housing, which is usually near the engine. Open the housing and remove the old air filter. Replace it with a new air filter that matches the make and model of your car. Close the housing and make sure it is securely fastened.
    +
    To replace a flat tire on your car, park it in a safe location away from traffic. Locate the spare tire and the jack in your car. Use the jack to lift the car off the ground, remove the flat tire, and replace it with the spare tire. Make sure to tighten the lug nuts securely before lowering the car back to the ground.
    +
    To replace the brake pads on your car, locate the brake caliper, which is usually near the wheel. Remove the caliper by loosening the bolts and pulling it away from the rotor. Remove the old brake pads and replace them with new ones that match the make and model of your car. Reinstall the caliper and tighten the bolts. Repeat the process for the other side of the car.
    +
    To replace the spark plugs in your car, locate the spark plug wires and remove them by pulling them off the spark plugs. Use a spark plug socket and ratchet to remove the old spark plugs. Replace them with new spark plugs that match the make and model of your car. Reinstall the spark plug wires by pushing them onto the spark plugs. Repeat the process for the other spark plugs in your car.
    +
    To replace a headlight bulb in your car, locate the headlight assembly, which is usually near the front of the car. Remove the old headlight bulb by turning it counterclockwise and pulling it out. Replace it with a new headlight bulb that matches the make and model of your car. Turn the new bulb clockwise to secure it in place.
    +
    To check the oil level in your car, park it on level ground and wait for the engine to cool down. Locate the oil dipstick, which is usually located near the engine. Pull out the dipstick, wipe it clean, reinsert it, and then pull it out again to check the oil level. The oil level should be between the two markers on the dipstick.
    +
    Use a tire pressure gauge to check the tyre pressure when the tires are cold, before driving your car. The recommended tire pressure can be found in your car's owner manual or on a sticker located in the driver's side door jamb. Remove the valve cap from the tire, press the tire pressure gauge onto the valve stem, and read the pressure on the gauge.

    It's recommended to change your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on your car's make and model. Check your car's owner manual for specific recommendations. If you frequently drive in extreme conditions, such as very hot or very cold weather, or dusty roads, you may need to change your oil more frequently.
